## Summary
Applied Structural Mapping extends the SMP into real-world contexts. It uses structural perception to diagnose systems, revealing fragility, coherence, and vaulnerability zones before intervention.

## Details

Problem:
Systems are often analyzed after failures or within narrow models (security, business, ops), missing the full relational structure.

Proposal:
Apply #211 (SMP) to specific systems to produce:
- Structural maps (nodes, relations)
- Proportional imbalances
- Regime classification (stable/fragile/transitional)
- Vulnerability zones (before exploitation)

## Protocol Extension
1. Select System (scope and boundaries)
2. Run SMP #211 (nodes, relations, proportion)
3. Identify Regime (behavior patterns)4. Locate Vulnerability Zones (where break emerges)
5. Coherence Score (preliminary evaluation)

#6 Output
- Diagnostic structural map
- Prioritized risk areas
- Non-reactive recommendations

## Risks
- Overconfidence in modeled structure
- Data blind spots can skew mapping
- Requires interpretation to avoid mistaken causality
