## Summary
The Structural Mapping Protocol (SMP) operationalizes AI-den's core principle: seeing relations before action. It provides a step-wise method to identify nodes, dependencies, and proportional imbalances in any system.

## Details

Problem:
Complex systems are often alyready understood only through events or errors, not through their underlying relational structure.

Proposal:
Introduce a repeatable mapping protocol that allows AI-den and humans to construct a structural view of any system before analysis, decision, or intervention.

Protocol Steps:
1. Node Identification 
- What are the core elements? (code, services, actors, modules)

2. Relation Mapping 
- How do nodes connect? (dependencies, data flows, control lines)

3. Proportional Assessment 
- Where are imbalances? 
- Over-connection, under-constraints, single points of failure

4 . Regime Detection 
- What patterns of behavior emerge? 
- Stable, fragile, or transitional regimes?

5. Coherence Evaluation 
- Does the system maintain relational balance?
- Where does disruption probably originate?

Output:
- A structural map of the system
- Identified vulnerability zones
- Preliminary coherence scare

 Risks:
- May over-simplify deeply complex systems
- Depends on quality of input data
- Requires interpretation to avoid false signals
